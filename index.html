Mobile Blood Drive

Why We Exist

Removing the Barrier Between Generosity and Action

Most people who want to donate blood never do — not from lack of will, but lack of opportunity. Donation centers require separate trips, scheduled appointments, and free time people simply don't have.

We solve that. By bringing a mobile blood drive bus to the events and places communities already love, we make it possible to save a life on the way to a concert, a farmer's market, or a school game.

Who We Are

Why I Started This

My name is Aidan Aframian, and I started The Ordinary Heroes Project because I believe that giving blood is the single greatest thing a person can do. It costs you nothing — no money, no lasting sacrifice — and yet it has the power to save up to three lives. In the Jewish tradition, we call this a mitzvah: a good deed. And I can think of few acts that carry more meaning while asking so little in return.

The first time I donated blood, I walked off that bus and felt something I didn't expect. Not just pride — possibility. I thought: if we could get everyone to do this, we could turn every ordinary person into a hero.

That thought became this project. I built The Ordinary Heroes Project to remove every barrier between a willing heart and a lifesaving act — by bringing blood drives directly to the communities where people already are.

How It Works

Four Steps. One Lifesaving Event.

We handle the complexity so organizers and donors don't have to.

01

We Partner with Hospitals

We establish relationships with licensed hospitals and blood centers who provide the trained medical staff and certified equipment for every drive.

02

We Connect with Event Organizers

Festivals, schools, corporations, places of worship — any gathering is an opportunity. We coordinate with organizers to schedule and promote the drive.

03

The Mobile Bus Arrives

A fully-equipped mobile blood drive bus parks at a designated spot at the event — no separate trip, no complicated setup, no burden on the host.

04

Community Members Donate

Attendees step on board, donate in under an hour, and go right back to enjoying the event — knowing they've just saved up to three lives.

FAQ

Common Questions

We partner with hospitals and blood centers who provide the medical staff and equipment. We then coordinate with event organizers to schedule a mobile blood drive bus at their event. Attendees can donate blood on-site without ever leaving the event.

No. The mobile bus is fully self-contained. You simply designate a parking spot, and our team handles everything else — check-in, the donation process, and post-drive cleanup.

The Ordinary Heroes Project does not collect blood. We coordinate and promote the drives. All blood collection is performed by licensed hospitals and blood centers whose trained medical professionals operate the mobile units.

The actual blood draw takes about 8–10 minutes. With check-in, health screening, and a brief recovery snack, the full process is typically under an hour — and well worth every minute.

We welcome requests from all types of organizations — festivals, schools, corporations, places of worship, sports venues, and more. Submit a request and our team will follow up to discuss feasibility and details.

A standard mobile blood drive bus needs roughly the space of two or three standard parking spots, with some clearance on the side for donor entry. Our team will assess your venue and make it work.

For qualifying community partners, there is no cost to host. Our mission is to remove every barrier to blood donation — including financial ones. Contact us to learn more about eligibility.
